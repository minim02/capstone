Annotated Bibliography

Introduction

This annotated bibliography supports my project on creating a multimodal digital portfolio that showcases my growth as a writer while integrating creative, professional, and academic dimensions. My research approach focused on locating sources that address digital portfolios, multimodal composition, revision processes, and the intersection of writing with professional identity and personal branding. Through this research, I learned how reflective practices, revision, and audience-conscious design are critical for conveying both learning and identity. These sources collectively inform my project by offering frameworks for constructing a portfolio that is not only a record of my work but also a curated representation of my evolving skills, self-presentation, and engagement with contemporary digital composing practices.

Baptista, D., Pereira, H., & Toledo, I. (2023, December 7)

Title: Perception of multimodal portfolios as a pedagogical strategy for learning writing in a digital context. Journal of Technologies Information and Communication. Link

Annotation: The authors are scholars in education and applied linguistics. Their study concludes that multimodal portfolios encourage engagement, creativity, digital literacy, and metacognitive awareness in the writing process. This aligns directly with my project’s goals to create a digital portfolio demonstrating writing growth through different media and genres.

Barnard, Josie. The Multimodal Writer. Bloomsbury Publishing, 6 Aug. 2019.

Annotation: Barnard explores how writers navigate multiple genres and digital platforms to shape their identities. This text is relevant to my project because it connects creative production to professional self-presentation. I will use Barnard’s framework to analyze how ePortfolios function as hybrid creative-professional texts.

Carter, Stacey. EPortfolios as a Platform for Evidencing Employability and Building Professional Identity: A Literature Review. 1 Jan. 2021

Annotation: Carter reviews scholarship on ePortfolios and their role in employability and professional identity. For my project, this source supports arguments about how digital composition extends beyond creative expression to encompass career development and self-representation.

Chin-Wen Chien. Influence of integration of multimodal writing portfolios into academic writing courses on novice researchers' academic writing. Journal of Applied Research in Higher Education 27 Nov. 2024; 16(5): 1389–1404. Link

Annotation: Chien’s study evaluates how integrating multimodal portfolios into academic courses influences novice researchers’ writing, reflection, and rhetorical awareness. This offers empirical validation that multimodal aspects support academic growth and guides reflective commentary on rhetorical engagement.

Gagich, M. (2020). An introduction to and strategies for multimodal composing. Link

Annotation: Gagich provides practical guidance for developing rhetorical awareness when composing in multiple modes. This will guide the structure of my multimodal pieces to ensure clear rhetorical purpose and audience awareness.

Gorbatov, Sergey, et al. “Get Noticed to Get Ahead: The Impact of Personal Branding on Career Success.” Frontiers in Psychology, vol. 10, no. 2662, 9 Dec. 2019. Link

Annotation: This article investigates the relationship between personal branding and career success. It provides a framework for discussing the intersection of creative and professional identity in multimodal writing and portfolios.

Gorbatov, Sergey, et al. “Personal Branding: Interdisciplinary Systematic Review and Research Agenda.” Frontiers in Psychology, vol. 9, no. 9, 21 Nov. 2018. Link

Annotation: This review explores personal branding across multiple disciplines, showing how digital portfolios function as both creative and professional identity tools. It provides vocabulary and frameworks for discussing self-presentation in multimodal authorship.

Rijlaarsdam, G., Couzijn, M., & van den Bergh, H. (n.d.). The study of revision as a writing process and as a learning-to-write process. Link

Annotation: This source provides a theoretical basis for treating revision as evidence of growth. It helps structure reflective commentary and highlight the evolution of my writing across the portfolio.

Weber, Karen. “Employer Perceptions of an Engineering Student’s Electronic Portfolio.” International Journal, vol. 8, no. 1, 2018, pp. 57–71. Link

Annotation: Weber explores employer perceptions of ePortfolios, highlighting the gap between student intentions and professional expectations. This informs how audience-conscious design and professional presentation function in my portfolio.

Yancey, K. (n.d.). Made not only in words: Composition in a new key. Link

Annotation: Yancey argues that writing must include digital and multimodal forms. Her insights justify including written, visual, and interactive modes as a legitimate, academic representation of writing identity.
